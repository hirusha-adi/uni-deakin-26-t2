What is a wireframe?
A wireframe is a blueprint for your product that outlines its structure and layout. It serves as a visual guide to communicate the overall design and functionality, helping stakeholders understand the general concepts and structure before development begins.

Purpose of Wireframes:
Planning and Refinement: Wireframes allow you to plan and refine the design of your application, ensuring that essential elements are properly organised and that the layout supports the intended functionality.
Visual Communication: They provide a clear visual representation of the user interface, helping team members and stakeholders understand the design direction and layout.
Types of Wireframes:
Low-Fidelity Wireframes: Basic, simple representations of the layout and structure. These are often used early in the design process to explore different concepts quickly.
Mid-Fidelity Wireframes: More detailed than low-fidelity wireframes, including more accurate placement of elements and interactions. Useful for refining the design and getting feedback on specific features.
High-Fidelity Wireframes: Detailed and close to the final design, including precise spacing, typography, and often some interactivity. These are used to finalise the design and provide a clearer idea of the end product.
