What is a navigation bar?
What is a Navigation Bar?

When your website includes a lot of content, it’s impractical to place everything on a single page. Designing a navigation bar in the header layout helps users easily access different sections of your site.

For example:

Horizontal Navigation Bar
This is a common method for allowing users to navigate between different pages or sections of content on your website.


Image Source: https://blavity.com/
Dropdown Navigation Menu
When you have multiple content pages within the same hierarchy, you can organise subitems under a parent item in the navigation menu. This allows users to access related pages or sections more efficiently by expanding the parent item to reveal the subitems.


Image Source: https://gestion.pe/
Hamburger Navigation Menu
On smaller devices, there may not be enough space to display all navigation bar items. The hamburger navigation menu is a popular solution for this. It uses a compact icon (typically three horizontal lines) to open a menu that reveals all the navigation items when clicked or tapped.


Image Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamburger_button
Vertical Sidebar Navigation Menu
This is an alternative method for displaying navigation options, allowing users to navigate through different sections of content. A vertical sidebar menu is typically positioned on the side of the page, offering a space-efficient way to organise and access various navigation items.


Image Source: https://www.behance.net/gallery/66772111/Maintenance-Page-Marvel-WordPress-Theme/modules/390625195
Footer Navigation Menu
When users don’t find important links in the header, they often look for additional options at the bottom of the page. A footer navigation menu provides a convenient place to include links to important pages or sections that users might need as they reach the end of your website.


Image Source: https://www.raywenderlich.com/
Activities
Start by creating your own navigation bar and view how it appears in a browser. This will help you understand its layout and functionality in a real-world context.
