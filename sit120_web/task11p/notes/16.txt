Learning Goals

Start a project plan
When working on your project, you should have a plan to present to stakeholders or team members. Consider including the following points:

Reasons (Why): Explain why the project is important and the problem it aims to solve.
Things (What): Outline what the project will include, such as features and deliverables.
Methods (How): Describe how you will execute the project, including the processes and tools you will use.
Additionally, assess how your product features compare to those of your competitors.

You might also include market analysis, such as relevant data or statistics, to demonstrate what makes your product unique and why it is worth developing.

Asset List plan
Creating a list of assets is crucial for understanding how they will be used and described. This helps ensure that others can easily read and utilise them.

For instance:

Asset Name: index.html

How to Be Used: Serves as the main connection point for all pages, including the general layout with elements such as the header, main content, and footer.
Details: File type is HTML.
Asset Name: logo.png
Purpose: The website's logo used in the header and other branding areas.
Details: PNG image file.
